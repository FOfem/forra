Power Transformers
Designs and Constructions

3


WORK REMOTELY AND DELIVER TO CLIENT LOCATION
A transformer is an electric device that transfers electric energy in a circuit at one alternating-current (AC) voltage level to one or multiple circuits at different alternating-current (AC) voltage level. If the Output voltage is higher than the Input voltage, this condition is known as STEP-UP. However, if the Output is lower than the Input Voltage, it is called STEP-DOWN.

The uses of transformers are endless. For example. Transformers are used to decrease the voltage of conventional power circuits to operate low-voltage devices as in the case of most power chargers and electric toys. Transformers are used to STEP-UP (Increase) the voltage from electric generators in power stations so that the electric power can be transmitted over longer distances. This is so because, transmitting high voltage with low current help to save power, but transmitting low voltage at high current would result in more power losses.

When electric current passes through the coil, it generate static magnetic fields on the area around it. Thus, a transformers can be used to generate magnetism. As the generated magnetic flux flows and cut across the turns of the secondary coils of the transformer, an electromagnetic induction occurs. From the Law of Thermodynamics we could see that, “Energy can neither be created nor be destroyed, but changes from one form to another.”

The change in polarity of the input (primary coil) alternating-current (AC) voltage collapse the built-up magnetic flux and alternate the magnetic polarities. Thus, this process induces alternating-current voltage on the another coil called the secondary coil or Output.

While the number of turns determines the voltage, the size (thickness) of the coil determines the current. This is true since a bigger coil size would have less resistivity to the flow of current when compared to a thin coil.

General Maintenance

5


ON SITE SERVICES
Maintenance is the regular checks and servicing, repairing or replacing of necessary devices, parts, accessories, or equipment which may range from machinery to residential or industrial building infrastructure or any supporting utilities, to keep it in operations and save cost of time and finance, even avoid possible accident which may lead to loss of life and property. Such maintenance may be any of the following.

  • Preventive Maintenance:Here, the equipment is checked and serviced regularly following a scheduled time table to prevent any future sudden breakdown.
  • Corrective Maintenance:Following a break down, malfunction or wearing out, the equipment is checked, serviced, repaired or replaced as the need may be.
  • Planned Maintenance:In this case, there is a scheduled downtime machine health-check to ensure it is working properly and fix any possible issues that could prevent the machine breakdown at an undesired time. Usually, such maintenance are handle by experts. This could also be refer to as Scheduled Maintenance or Planned Preventive Maintenance.
  • Breakdown Maintenance:This is the culture were things are fixed only after they breakdown. It is also known as the Reactive Maintenance Strategy. The disadvantage of this practice is that the damage resulting could be more than imagined.
  • Predictive Maintenance:This is a method were the machine continue to perform until certain conditions are observed or noticed, necessitating the need for servicing. Since maintenance are ally done when there is a need, this form of maintenance is considered cost effective.
  • Condition-Based Maintenance:This form of maintenance is classified under Predictive Maintenance. However, it is the form of maintenance that is done only when the need arises. With the introduction of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in technologies, the system able to monitor the conditions of the items. Hence the name, “Condition Based Monitoring” (CBM). Thus, when there are one or more indicators that an equipment is deteriorating or failing, the CBM maintenance would be done.
